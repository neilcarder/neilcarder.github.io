ARCHIVES OF UNFINISHED WORK

The project seeks to embody aspects of the fundamental unfinalisability at the heart of human interaction and creation. The basis for exploration is the paradoxical relationship between the self and the other, that we are all “subjects in incompleteness.” This dialogue between self and other has necessary components and useless components. Much like human emotion, understanding can be only to a certain degree: some parts are never understood. An acceptance of these unfinished aspects of human understanding reduces frustration.

The project for an archives of unfinished plays, music and dance, connects these themes with the human creative processes. It does not store failed attempts, but lost aspirations. As a sort of architectural sphinx, the project plays with the disposes of their own potential; it leads them in random directions, changes the exit paths and forces reflection upon those storing the incomplete creative work. It also plays with the lost potential of the creative work, inviting (partial) completion by others, and exposing the result to the elements and the eyes of the world.
The concept drawing above focuses on the border. There is visually more interaction at the border. This represents the conflict between self and other; an example could be ones own ideals versus the world's current state. Or a conflict between two characters. An important aspect of the frawing this that although there is interaction and conflict at the border, the basic essence of self and other is virtually the same.

HOUSING & COMMUNITY CENTRE
ARCHITECTURAL CONCEPT: WEAVING

A current issue with immigration in Canada is a lack of immigrants’ integration with current societies, communities and technologies of contemporary Canada. Whether an immigrant decides to move to Canada for cultural integration, a wealthy lifestyle or other reasons, full integration will increase the progress of the individual and the community.


The goal to be achieved is full (while respecting cultures and beliefs) integration into a society. This raised the idea of weaving; weaving two or more strands, fabrics or materials elegantly into a uniform fabric. The fabric creates a unified entity while maintaining its individual elements; there is a vast amount of connections in a fabric. This is the cause of its appeal and structure.

THE PROGRAM

The goal of this project is to create a research centre for the town of Almonte in Ontario, Canada. The type of research project was the choice of the student; my research centre is built for the purpose of developing urban wind turbines due to many issues with large commercial turbines placed around rural areas. Almonte is a small town with a potential to grow. This mirrors the current state of urban designed renewable energy.


Issues associated with commercial wind turbines include gears in the generators malfunctioning, birds being killed by turning blades, disturbance of people and animals (caused by noise and shadow movement) and built-up ice being thrown from the large blades during winter season. Due to these issues, the constant goal of this proposed research building is to maximize efficiency and reduce disturbance issues of urban wind turbines which would reduce the demand of commercial turbines. The building will be a necessary asset for the teaching of current turbine technology, production of prototypes and testing of prototypes.
